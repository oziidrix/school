Microeconomics

The Microeconomics course provides students with a foundational understanding of economic principles at the individual level. Students will learn about consumer behavior, producer behavior, market structures, pricing strategies, and resource allocation.

The course is ideal for individuals interested in understanding how individuals and firms make decisions in resource allocation and how markets operate under different conditions.

Course Requirements:

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Basic understanding of mathematics
  • Interest in economics and analytical thinking

Waiting Time for Acceptance: 2-4 weeks
